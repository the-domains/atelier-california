Alessandro di Mariano di Vanni Filipepi, known asSandro Botticelli(

Italian:

[ˈsandro bottiˈtʃɛlli]; c. 1445[1]– May 17, 1510), was anItalian painterof the EarlyRenaissance. He belonged to theFlorentine Schoolunder the patronage ofLorenzo de' Medici, a movement thatGiorgio Vasariwould characterize less than a hundred years later in hisVitaof Botticelli as a "golden age". Botticelli's posthumous reputation suffered until the late 19th century; since then, his work has been seen to represent the linear grace of Early Renaissance painting.

Among Botticelli's best-known works areThe Birth of VenusandPrimavera.
